The real story

Meet
Sunday Rax

“Around here, we do not just survive. We SOAR.”

Sunday Rax is not your average bio-page kind of woman.

She is a wife, mother, ordained pastor, author, entrepreneur, financial strategist, tax professional, speaker, mentor, and builder. She wears a lot of hats—but not for show. She has lived, learned, cried, prayed, built, rebuilt, and now she helps other people do the same.

Sunday is the founder of Rax Financial & Tax Solutions, LLC, where she helps individuals, families, business owners, nonprofits, and entrepreneurs get their money, taxes, business structure, and paperwork together. She breaks finances down in real language, with real strategy, so people can stop guessing and start building.

Her business journey did not start with everything lined up perfectly. Sunday went from working a 9–5, barely making it from check to check, to quitting her job in a spur-of-the-moment leap of faith to pursue her own tax business. In her very first tax season, she made her previous annual salary in just four months.

She is also the Founder and Lead Pastor of SOAR Global Ministries—a ministry for the ones who are tired of pretending everything is fine; the misfits, mantle-carriers, in-betweeners, and people marked by God but mishandled by people.

Through preaching, prayer, prophetic counseling, mentorship, deliverance, outreach, and discipleship, Sunday helps people surrender the pain, obey God’s voice, activate their purpose, and rebuild their lives from the ground up.

Sunday knows what it feels like to be stretched, overlooked, betrayed, exhausted, and still have to get up. To build while healing, pray while tired, lead while learning, and show up even when life tried to sit her down.

He healed.He restored.He redirected.He rebuilt.

Now Sunday uses her voice, wisdom, testimony, and strategy to help others rise too. Her style is bold, warm, funny, honest, and full of faith. She will pray with you, laugh with you, tell you the truth, help you fix the paperwork, and remind you that you are not done yet.

Before the brands, books, ministry, and business moves, there was a season where Sunday knew what it felt like to struggle—to carry dreams bigger than her bank account.
